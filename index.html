Addition Of Whole Numbers

Natural Numbers

Natural numbers are the numbers we use for counting. They start from 1 and go on forever.
Examples: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, … (and so on).


Whole Numbers

Whole numbers are just like natural numbers, but they also include zero (0).
Examples: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, … (and so on).




Fact: All natural numbers are whole numbers, but not all whole numbers are natural numbers.


Addition in a Row: Let’s Have Fun Adding Numbers!

Adding numbers can be easy and exciting! Let’s look at how we can add numbers step-by-step, one after another.

Addition Without Carrying

When no carrying is needed, addition is straightforward.
Let’s use 34 + 12 as an example:

  1. Separate the Place Values
    34 = 3 tens and 4 ones.

    12 = 1 ten and 2 ones.
  2. Add the Ones Add the ones directly:
    4 + 2 = 6 ones.
  3. Add the Tens
    Add the tens:
    3 + 1 = 4 tens (40).
  4. Combine the Results
    Put the tens and ones together:

    So, 34 + 12 = 46.

Addition With Carrying:

Now let’s add 47 + 26, where we need to carry over a number.

  1. Separate the Place Values
    47 = 4 tens and 7 ones.

    26 = 2 tens and 6 ones.
  2. Add the Ones
    Add the ones: 7 + 6 = 13 ones.

    Since 13 is more than 10, we carry over 1 ten.
  3. Add the Tens (Including the Carry)
    Add the tens: 4 + 2 + 1 (the carry) = 7 tens.
  4. Combine the Results
    So, 47 + 26 = 73.

Addition by Breaking in Parts

Breaking numbers into parts makes addition easier by focusing on place values . This method helps in solving large additions step by step.

Steps to Add by Breaking in Parts:

  1. Break each number based on place values.
  2. Add each place value separately.
  3. Combine the sums to get the final answer.

Example:

Break each number based on place values:
213 = 200 + 10 + 3
214 = 200 + 10 + 4

Add the hundreds, tens and ones separately.

Combine all sums:
400 + 20 + 7 = 427
So, 213 + 214 = 427.

Addition of Numbers Ending with Zero

Numbers ending with zero are 10, 20, 30, 100, 200, etc. Adding such numbers is very simple.

Example 1: 200 + 500

Step 1: Ignore the zeros and add 2 + 5 = 7.
Step 2: Attach two zeros back → 700.

Thus, 200 + 500 = 700

Example 2: 400 + 80 + 20

Step 1: Add 80 and 20 first → 80 + 20 = 100.
Step 2: Add 400 + 100 → 500.

Thus, 400 + 80 + 20 = 500

Properties of Addition

Addition follows several fundamental properties that help simplify calculations.

1. Commutative Property
The order in which two numbers are added does not change the sum. That is, a+b=b+a

Example:
5 + 3 = 3 + 5
8 = 8

2. Associative Property
The way numbers are grouped in addition does not change the sum. That is, (a+b)+c=a+(b+c)

Example:
(2 + 3) + 4 = 2 + (3 + 4)
5 + 4 = 2 + 7
9 = 9

3. Identity Property
The sum of any number and zero is the number itself. That is, a+0=0+a=a.

Example:
9 + 0 = 0 + 9 = 9
